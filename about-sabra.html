About sabaragamuwa
natural paradise of sri lanka

Your're here : Home / About Sabaragamuwa

Nestled in the southwest region of Sri Lanka, Sabaragamuwa Province is a hidden gem that blends ancient treasures, stunning natural beauty, and a touch of adventure. Known as the "Province of Gems," Sabaragamuwa invites travelers to embark on a journey of discovery, from exploring its rich history to immersing themselves in the wonders of the rainforest.

Historical Allure Sabaragamuwa's past is etched into its landscape. Explore the ruins of the Kuragala Buddhist Monastery, dating back to the 2nd century BC, and marvel at the impressive Dedigama Kota Vehera, a large stupa steeped in history. Discover traditional Sri Lankan hospitality at rest houses like the Algama Mahawaththa Ambalama, which once sheltered weary travelers. These historical sites offer a glimpse into the cultural heritage of the region.

Natural Splendors Step into the primeval beauty of Sinharaja Forest Reserve, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This lush rainforest teems with endemic flora and fauna, offering exhilarating hikes and the chance to spot rare wildlife. The cascading Bopath Ella waterfall, with its distinctive leaf-like shape, provides a picturesque setting for a refreshing swim. Embark on a wildlife safari in Udawalawe National Park, renowned for its large elephant population, and spot leopards, crocodiles, and an array of bird species.

Cultural Immersion Sabaragamuwa's vibrant local festivals provide a window into Sri Lankan culture. Don't miss the spectacular Esala Perahera in Ratnapura, featuring elaborately decorated elephants, dancers, and drummers. The Sabaragamuwa Dance Festival celebrates the region's unique dance traditions. Sabaragamuwa is a province that captivates with its diverse offerings. Whether you seek historical treasures, natural wonders, thrilling adventures, or cultural immersion, this enchanting region of Sri Lanka promises an unforgettable experience.
